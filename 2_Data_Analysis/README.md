## Introduction

Our most valuable asset is often time. This project attempts to solve a few problems and save as much time as possible. This dataset shows the optimal job skills required and requested for by employers for data job seekers. This helps job seekers understand what are the top skills top employers request and shows data workers what skills could land them more pay. In this way, people looking for jobs can ensure they are well-equipped to enter the workforce with the valuable skills employers are seeking. And as a bonus, the employer can make sure they're hiring someone who has the correct skills for the job. 

### Questions to Analyze
In this project I was trying to firgure out:
1. Do more skills get you more pay?
2. What's the salary for data jobs in different locations?
3. What are the top skills of data workers?
4. What is the top pay for the top 10 skills?

### Excel Skills Used
In this project I started to use more advanced Excel skills like Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, DAX (Data Analysis Expressions), Power Query, and Power Pivot. Just about all of these tools were brand new to me to learn and it became clear that Excel is so much more than just a good way of housing data. 

### Data Jobs Dataset
Like in project 1, this is built on the dataset provided by Luke Barousse in his Excel for Data Analytics Course. This data includes information like the job titles, job locations, pay information, and skills required. 

### More Skills = More Pay?
Power Query was used to perform the tasks of Extract, Transform, and Locate on the data for the necessary parameters. This was very helpful in understanding how a large dataset with tons of information can be simplified and made into usable data for visualization purposes. 

#### Analysis
In simple terms, Engineer type jobs require more skills but don't always mean more pay for those skills. As expected, Senior roles pay the most but also require more skills. But overall, higher paying job roles are going to require more specialized skills.

### Top Skills Require for Data Analysts in the U.S.
The next logical step in seeing what jobs have a great return on the investment of skills, is to try to dial in to what specific skills each job requires. For this, I picked to look specifically at Data Analysts jobs in the U.S. 

This dataset was then used to build out a data visualization tool to help people understand what are those top skills required for someone who is seeking out to become a data anaylst in the U.S. 

#### Analysis 
Someone who is trying to get a job in the world of data analytics in the U.S. would greatly benifit from focusing their efforts on learning skills like SQL, Excel, data visualization tools like Tableau or PowerBI, along with some coding languages like Python or R. Knowing Microsoft basics like Word and Powerpoint might be beneficial to work within a company but these top skills are where a student could make an impact and enter the job pool ready to compete with other people who have already established themselves in the industry.
